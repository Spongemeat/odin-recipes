Okonomiyaki

It can be hard for Vegans and Vegetarians to enjoy Japanese food due to the reliance on fish stock in almost every dish. This is a recipe that doesn't use any fish stock to create a Japanese staple comfort food: Okonomiyaki (Cabbage pancake) Junky, healthy and vegetarian all at the same time, this is a recipe sure to satisfy anyone!

Ingredients

  • Eggs (can be substituted with flax yolk)
  • Onion Stock Powder
  • Water
  • White Flour
  • Soysauce
  • Flathead Cabbage
  • Carrots
  • Smoked Tofu
  • Mushrooms
  • Bulldog Sauce
  • Kewpie Mayonnaise
  • Aonori
  • Beni Shoga

Steps

  1. Shred the cabbage and carrots thinly with a mandolin or knife
  2. Slice mushrooms and tofu into thin slices
  3. In a large bowl, mix cold water, white flour and eggs (substitute flax). Whisk in soy sauce and onion powder. Should be pancake batter consistency
    • It is a good idea to have a separate, extra bowl with a little extra of this mixture for later
  4. Mix shredded cabbage and carrots into mixture
  5. Heat a non-stick pan to medium-high
  6. Coat with oil and lay down mushrooms, circumference of desired okonomiyaki size
  7. Pour mixture on top of mushrooms, similar to shape and size of a thick pancake
  8. Press down okonomiyaki with a dish for a few seconds to establish shape
    • Pour a little bit of extra mixture if you made some onto okonomiyaki
  9. Add tofu strips on top
  10. After 10 minutes, or once okonomiyaki is solid, flip and continue to cook for another 10 minutes
  11. Slide onto a plate and top with Bulldog Sauce, Kewpie, Aonori, and Beni Shoga generously and serve
