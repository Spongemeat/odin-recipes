Vegan Miso Soup

It can be hard for Vegans and Vegetarians to enjoy Japanese food due to the reliance on fish stock in almost every dish.

This is a recipe that doesn't use any fish stock to create a delicious bowl of miso soup that anyone can enjoy

The use of a darker miso paste as well as onion and shiitake stock more than makes up for the lack of dashi

Ingredients

  • Mixed or Red Miso Paste
  • Onion stock powder
  • Dried Shiitake mushrooms
  • Dried Kombu
  • Soft Tofu
  • Wakame
  • (optional) Fingerling Potatoes
  • (optional) Small Onion

Steps

  1. Cut the potatoes and soft tofu into small, bite sized pieces
  2. Cut onion in half, and slice thinly
  3. Add water to a pot with a piece of kombu, shiitake, potatoes, tofu and onions, and bring to a boil.
  4. Lower heat to a simmer once boiling
  5. Add onion stock powder and continue to simmer until potatoes are cooked through
  6. Once potatoes are cooked, lower heat to low and add miso paste
    • do not let soup boil after this step as it will alter the taste of the miso
  7. Allow soup to heat on low for 10 minutes
  8. Add some dried wakame to your bowl and serve
    • Wakame will rehytdrate quickly once broth is added to bowl
